Digital Reverie: The Mesmerizing Art of Sam Spratt

February 06, 2024

Who Sam Spratt ?

Sam is a New York based artist working out of his studio in the city. Sam’s pursuit is to make human art with human values about our stumbles, realizations, and fight to engage in a shared reality. Through intricacy at all levels, he seeks to create art within the digital space we actually operate in today that preserves the tactility, code, and values of the space we move through physically. Through focusing that technique towards a human story, his goal is to provide a bridge for the natural world as it extends into a digital network.

How does he create these artworks ?

For the last decade, he has been honing his skills in utilizing digital mediums to recreate traditional painting techniques. His artwork has found its way into various realms such as games, films, TV, music, books, comics, magazines, and theater, touching upon a wide spectrum of creative industries. Nevertheless, his primary goal in adopting this technique is to imbue the digital space we navigate today with a tactile quality reminiscent of our physical surroundings.

His notable works include album art for various artists and companies.

Everybody (Album Cover)

The inspiration for this detailed album cover came about when Sam received a call from Robert Bryson Hall II (Logic) who was in the Louvre at the time. “The wedding at Cana by Paolo Veronese” is the painting the rapper had his eye’s on. The painting has 130 human figures, Robert explained that this would be the inspiration for the cover.

He’s done artworks for most of Logic's discography on albums such as “Under Pressure”, “The Incredible True Story”, “No pressure”, “Bobby Tarantino” & most recently “Ultra 85”.
